AV Club Reviews

The AV Club has published over 16,000 TV reviews since 2007, assigning each reviewed episode a letter grade (from A to F). Which shows does AV Club like or dislike? Which do they like more than other reviewers? Do they have a preference towards certain kinds of shows? Directors? Are some reviewers easier graders than others?

I decided to try to find out by scraping the review data by their site. I matched it up with IMDB data, which had more information about directors, actors, air-dates and genre classifications, as well as community ratings to use as benchmarks.

Below are some visualizations for exploring or analyzing AV Club Review data by show, episode, director, genre, or reviewer.

Here, each show/series reviewed by AV Club is represented by a bar. Scores represent average grades across all reviews (where letter grades are assigned corresponding numeric values, evenly distributed from 0 to 100). The IMDB community score was used as a benchmark to better understand which shows AV Club reviewed more or less favorably, relative to public opinion. The "Net Score" is simply the difference between average AV Club score and the average IMDB score.

Click on any show for more details.


The average review score for each season of a show is represented here (hover over any bar for individual episodes). The plot below the barchart shows the net score for each season, relative to IMDB scores.


Distance from the mid-line on these bubble plots indicates disagreement between AV Club reviewers and the IMDB community score. Those shows or genres that are closer to the bottom-right corner are viewed less favorably by AV Club reviewers than the general public, while those closer to the top-left are viewed more favorably.


Here, directors are shown using the same bubble plot, with distance from the mid-line representing preference for or against a given director by the AV Club reviewer.

Directors are only included if they've had at least 25 episodes of their work reviewed by AV Club across at least two different shows/series.


I wanted to get a sense of AV Club's easiest/toughest reviewers, while adjusting for varying quality of the content that they were reviewing. To do this, I looked only at seasons of TV shows with multiple reviewers, assuming that quality would be more consistent within a season than across seasons or shows. I then looked at each reviewer who reviewed at least two episodes within that season and compared their scores to the scores of their peers, assigning them a "bias" score for the season's reviews. These bias scores were then averaged across all seasons that they shared with another reviewer. Only those who shared at least two seasons across at least two different shows were included.

Nine points would represent a full grade, so the greatest bias found in either direction was just over a third of a grade.
